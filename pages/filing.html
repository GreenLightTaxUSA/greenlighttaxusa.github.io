Filing

Filing as Single

Required personal Information:

  • Social Security Number (SSN) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN).
  • Proof of income (e.g., W-2s or 1099s).
  • Income Records:
    • Wages and salary (W-2).
    • Interest and dividends (1099-INT, 1099-DIV).
    • Unemployment benefits (1099-G).
    • Investment or stock gains/losses (1099-B).
  • Deductions and Credits:
    • Student loan interest (Form 1098-E).
    • Tuition and education expenses (Form 1098-T).
    • Charitable donations.
    • Health savings account (HSA) contributions (Form 5498-SA).

Filing as Head of Household

Additional Requirements:

  • Proof of qualifying dependent (SSN or ITIN for the dependent).
  • Proof of more than 50% support for household expenses (e.g., rent, utilities, groceries).
  • Income Records:
    • All income types listed above.
    • Child support or alimony (if applicable).
  • Deductions and Credits:
    • Child and dependent care expenses (Form 2441).
    • Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC).
    • Child Tax Credit (CTC).
    • Medical expenses (if itemizing).

Filing as a Family with Dependents

Dependents’ Information:

  • SSN or ITIN for all dependents.
  • Childcare provider details (name, address, and Taxpayer Identification Number).
  • Income Records:
    • All forms of income for both parents (W-2, 1099s).
    • Dependent income (if applicable).
  • Deductions and Credits:
    • Child Tax Credit and Additional Child Tax Credit.
    • Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC).
    • Adoption expenses (if applicable).
    • Education credits (Form 8863 for the American Opportunity Credit or Lifetime Learning Credit).
    • Childcare expenses (Form 2441).

Filing as a Business Owner

Business Information:

  • Employer Identification Number (EIN), if applicable.
  • Business name and address.
  • Income Records:
    • 1099 forms for contractor income.
    • Profit and loss statement.
    • Bank statements or accounting records.
  • Expense Records:
    • Business expenses (e.g., rent, utilities, office supplies, advertising).
    • Vehicle expenses (mileage log or actual expenses).
    • Home office deduction (if applicable).
  • Deductions and Credits:
    • Self-employment tax deduction.
    • Retirement contributions (e.g., SEP IRA).
    • Health insurance premiums (for self-employed individuals).
  • Other Documentation:
    • Estimated tax payments made during the year (Form 1040-ES).

General Checklist for All Filers

  • Prior year’s tax return (for reference).
  • Proof of identity (e.g., driver’s license, passport).
  • Bank account information for direct deposit of refund.
  • Receipts for deductions (e.g., medical expenses, charitable donations, education).
  • IRS forms and publications for specific circumstances (available at IRS.gov).
